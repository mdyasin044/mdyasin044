About

Engineering meets research

I'm a software engineer and ML researcher based in Tempe, Arizona, with a B.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET). I graduated in 2019.

After BUET, I joined Samsung Research Bangladesh, where I spent six years building production systems for Samsung's medical ultrasound machines, Knox enterprise platform, and health applications. I wore many hats — backend engineer, UI architect, ML prototype designer, DSA mentor — and shipped across C#, .NET, React, NodeJS, and Swift.

Alongside my engineering work, I pursued applied ML research including a diffusion-model-based super-resolution system for ultrasound images and an undergraduate thesis on spatio-temporal air quality prediction for Dhaka. I'm passionate about building systems that are not just functional, but intelligent.

Research Output

Research Experience

Applied ML research spanning medical imaging, environmental forecasting, and NLP — from Samsung R&D to undergraduate thesis work.

2024
Samsung Research BD · R&D
Deep Learning-based Super-Resolution of Ultrasound Images (SR3-style Diffusion Model)
Md. Yasin · Samsung Research Bangladesh

Implemented a denoising diffusion probabilistic model with an SR3-style U-Net architecture to enhance low-resolution medical ultrasound images. Achieved PSNR ≈ 32 dB and SSIM ≈ 0.93. Compared quantitatively and qualitatively against CNN and GAN-based baselines, showing competitive performance.

Details
2019
Undergrad Thesis · BUET
Time Series Based Air Quality Prediction Model for Spatio-Temporal Data
Md. Yasin · Supervised by Dr. Anindya Iqbal, BUET

Collected and analyzed five years of urban air quality data from Dhaka. Developed and benchmarked forecasting models — SARIMAX, RNN, CNN, MLP — to predict PM concentrations. Achieved lowest MAE (5.48) and RMSE (7.98) with SARIMAX, outperforming deep learning baselines. Demonstrated potential for urban policy and health risk mitigation.

Thesis
2019
Academic Project · BUET
Email Clustering Using Doc2Vec
Md. Yasin · BUET CSE Department

Implemented unsupervised clustering of emails using Doc2Vec embeddings combined with K-Means to group semantically similar messages. Used Gensim, scikit-learn, and pandas for vectorization and analysis on a subset of the Enron email dataset.
